gemeinschaft & gesellschaft

factory turned to apts w/ choices from modes of shared living

location | Xi'a, China
type | academic
role | Concept 50%, detail 70%, drawing 80% with Jiaxuan Tang
date | Apr to Jun, 2018
Tönnies invented the terms Gemeinschaft and Gesellschaft (community and society) to denote the pre- & post-industrial societies. Gemeinschaft relates to personal relations, bonding, and responsibilities, and Gesellschaft, the modern, impersonal, and the random.
While Tönnies hankered for the old Gemeinschaft, we also appreciate the erratic encounters, the wide networks, and endless opportunities in the Gesellschaft. We believe people have a choice - whether to “bond” in Gemeinschaft, to “wander” in Gesellschaft, or to just be on one’s own.
This concept is realised in the renovation of a textile factory. The factory used to have a grid structure: the column grid, and the roof made of replicated slopes. We use this grid, densify it, and created different modes of shared living on different floors.

In the vicinity of the textile plants have been two types of shared living: the Danwei dwellings that peaked from the 1950s to the 1980s, and the urban villages that emerged since the 1980s. These correspond with the narrative of Tönnies regarding the Gemeinschaft and the Gesellschaft. The Danwei is a work-live mixed entity where employees are deeply integrated in the hierarchical collective community.
3. a choice to different types of shared living
diagram of four levels relating to different modes of sharing
Although Tönnies himself might have hankered for something close to the Gemeinschaft, we also cherish the flexibility and possibilities in the Gesellschaft fluxes. People should have the opportunity to choose from whatever mode they want any time they want.
