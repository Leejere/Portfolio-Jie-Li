intergrated linear town as channel, link, & stimulator
How effective was planning to guide urbanisation in the sprawling Beijing? While the built-up area is subjected to planning control, economic hot zones continued the slower and concentric profusion or followed other robust economic cores, highlighting the importance of economic basis in new town creation. Based on this conclusion, and inspired by a strand of prosperous towns stretching to the northwest driven by Zhongguancun Science Park, I propose a linear new town in the northeast thhat uses multi-modal transport to attain full integration with its base (another dynamic science park), and to receive "spill-overs." Meanwhile, its elongated shape and linear transport pattern guaranteed inside density, avoiding sprawl and acting as economic stimulator to a much larger area.
factory-turned to youth apartments offering choices to various modes of shared living
Tönnies invented the terms Gemeinschaft and Gesellschaft (community and society) to denote the pre- & post-industrial societies. Gemeinschaft relates to personal relations, bonding, and responsibilities, and Gesellschaft, the modern, impersonal, and the random. While Tönnies hankered for the old Gemeinschaft, we also appreciate the erratic encounters, the wide networks, and endless opportunities in the Gesellschaft. We believe people have a choice - whether to “bond” in Gemeinschaft, to “wander” in Gesellschaft, or to just be on one’s own. This concept is realised in the renovation of a textile factory. The factory used to have a grid structure: the column grid, and the roof made of replicated slopes. We use this grid, densify it, and created different modes of shared living on different floors.
memorial galleries underneath bombed colonial governorate building
Sitting by the Tigris in Baghdad's core where Abbasid and Ottoman heritages are found, the bombed British governorate building was not an Iraqi native, but itself the product of colonialization. Then it was destroyed by yet another force—the 2003 war. The complex history resists simple architectural narrative, and I made an informed design to respect and highlight the stacking historical layers. I retain the ruin-like features of the bombed site, leaving space for reflection. The former courtyard-like structure are reconstructed underground as galleries or workshops. Thus an interface emerges between the ruined and the refined, the light and the dark, and between implicit and explicit memorial displays.
