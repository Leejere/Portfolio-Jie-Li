the bridge

renovation of railway danwei for social, human-land and chronological unities

location | Yong'an, China
type | academic
role | Individual work
advisor | Feng Song of Peking University
date | Feb to June, 2020
This design emerges from addressing multiple goals (economic, ecological, historical) in the renovation of a declining district on the border of the built environment. Yong'an was once the hub of what used to be the only railway line in the province. The railway Danwei was once a prosperous area, separate and comparable with the town core. The waning importance of the railway has brough dilapidation of this area.
My design seeks to reach social, human-land and chronological unities. (1) Social unity. The old station is reused as a transit station after adding a feeder that connects to the new railway station, and becomes the economic driver; meanwhile, a public space system is devised to allow all sorts and levels of public discourse. (2) Human-land unity. I use the reverse planning method by denoting the "ecological infrastructure" prior to adding function programs. The ecological infrastructure becomes the pivot of the public space system. (3) Chronological unity. I retain the old station, the Workers' Club and parts of old Danwei housing, and integrate them in the public space system. Part of the railway is also used for recreation.

2. declines
Decline of former railway danwei area
Danweis were mixed work-live complexes from 1949 to 1990s. Massive land on the urban fringes was allocated wholesale to Danweis who took care of their own construction. As a symbol of China’s industrialization age, since 1990’s, Danweis went to declines; Many of them were redeveloped to housing.
