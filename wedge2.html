the wedge

intergrated linear town as channel, link, & stimulator

location | Beijing, China
type | academic
role | 1st author of research; individual design
advisor | Feng Song of Peking University
date | Mar to Oct 2020
How effective was planning to guide urbanisation in the sprawling Beijing? While the built-up area is subjected to planning control, economic hot zones continued the slower and concentric profusion or followed other robust economic cores, highlighting the importance of economic basis in new town creation. Based on this conclusion, and inspired by a strand of prosperous towns stretching to the northwest driven by Zhongguancun Science Park, I propose a linear new town in the northeast thhat uses multi-modal transport to attain full integration with its base (another dynamic science park), and to receive "spill-overs." Meanwhile, its elongated shape and linear transport pattern guaranteed inside density, avoiding sprawl and acting as economic stimulator to a much larger area.

13. circulation
3-D transport overlayering
In order to achieve total inner integration for all types of travelers, the design makes the following efforts:
1. The two north-south freeways are guaranteed to be uninterrupted. All intersections with these two freeways are accomplished with overpasses.
2. East-west by-passing traffic must not interfere with the intergration of the wedge. All must pass through viaducts or underground tunnels.
3. The Walkway and Bikeway must not be interrupted by automobile traffic. All traffic that cuts through the Walkway or Bikeway must be pass through tunnels.
The subway is kept underground. Trains must not run on the ground, as this would split up the wedge into thinner strips. The subway tunnels are usually 12 meters below ground level, sonot to interfere with automobile tunnels.

Section E-E'

15. center spine as pivot
Ground-floor plan
The Walkway and Bikways are the central spines, which are not only for transportation purposes, but themselves broadened into the pivot of the public space system.
Sitting beside or wrapped by the central spines are the important public or commercial buildings which are facilitated with direct access to the spines.
