autonomous vehicles transformation

gateway & offices in pioneering autonomous vehicles campus

location | Philadelphia, PA
type | academic
role | Individual part in group work
date | Oct to Dec 2022
The autonomous vehicle (AV), if widely adopted, could fundamentally change the built environment. This design is premised on the assumption that shared AV fleets and autonomous buses will be the primary means of transport in the future, with a smaller number of privately owned AV’s. This could mean narrower streets, much less parking, densification, and mobility hubs for AVs dotted around the city. Another challenging part is the transition period where the adoption of AVs is not high enough so that AV’s and non-AVs must co-exist, resulting AVs not able to perform at their best.
Navy Yard, Philadelphia, is a former naval base and now a business campus relatively cut off from the rest of the city. While making plans to attract more people and businesses to the campus, it is also experimenting with an AV solution. As a group, we made a comprehensive plan for Navy Yard that would incoporate the use of AVs, both in the near future (transition) and in longer future. The portfolio shows my individual part in the comprehensive plan: the design of the gateway, and a concept design of office buildings in the age of AVs.
