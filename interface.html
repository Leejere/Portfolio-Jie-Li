the interface

memorial galleries underneath bombed colonial governorate building

location | Baghdad, Iraq
type | academic
role | Individual work
date | Jul to Aug 2018
Sitting by the Tigris in Baghdad's core where Abbasid and Ottoman heritages are found, the bombed British governorate building was not an Iraqi native, but itself the product of colonialization. Then it was destroyed by yet another force—the 2003 war.
The complex history resists simple architectural narrative, and I made an informed design to respect and highlight the stacking historical layers. I retain the ruin-like features of the bombed site, leaving space for reflection. The former courtyard-like structure are reconstructed underground as galleries or workshops. Thus an interface emerges between the ruined and the refined, the light and the dark, and between implicit and explicit memorial displays.
