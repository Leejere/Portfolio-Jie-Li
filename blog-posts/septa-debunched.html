## Why doing this project?
Knowing when buses are coming, riders plan their trips without stress. Unfortunately, disruptions occur and affect some buses more than others, leading to bunching, where two buses arrive at a stop consecutively, accompanied by subsequent gapping, where riders have to wait a long time after the bunched buses left. This should not happen too often to a reliable bus service.
## What we did
We were fortunate to obtain data from SEPTA, Philadelphia's primary trainsit provider and studied what causes bunching and what are the measures to take, both long-term (infrastructure-wise) and short-term (operation-wise). Read the [markdown](https://leejere.github.io/otis-corridor). Yihong Hu was the primary author of the markdown.
## What is the app?
I was the primary author of the app, a proof-of-concept of the operational approach, whereby we predict bunching on the fly (up to 20 stops beforehand) to fine-tune the speeds of the buses to prevent before it happens. Visit the app [here](https://leejere.github.io/debunch-septa-app/).
Using the app, the user can select a route, direction, and a particular bus trip using real-time map display. Then, the user can view the prediction results on the panel.
